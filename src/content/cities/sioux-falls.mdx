## How to Choose a Forensic Accountant in Sioux Falls

- **Verify active credentials, not just listed ones.** CFF (Certified in Financial Forensics) and CFE (Certified Fraud Examiner) holders must maintain continuing education. Ask for current CPE completion — a lapsed credential is a cross-examination target waiting to happen.
- **Match the credential to the case type.** A CFF is the right call for fraud investigation and litigation support; a CVA or ABV handles business valuation disputes — divorce proceedings, shareholder conflicts, or buy-sell disagreements. South Dakota's treatment of marital business interests has specific implications for valuation methodology that a generalist may miss.
- **Ask how many times they've testified in South Dakota courts.** Daubert standards apply in federal court, but Minnehaha County Circuit Court judges have their own expectations for expert report formatting and deposition conduct. Local courtroom experience is not optional.
- **Request a redacted sample report.** Any qualified forensic accountant can show you a prior expert report with identifying details removed. If they hesitate, move on. The report is the deliverable — you need to know what you're buying before signing the engagement letter.
- **Confirm availability before you retain them.** Sioux Falls is a small market, which means the best practitioners are frequently booked 6–8 weeks out. If your court date is 90 days away, ask about scheduling on day one.

> **Pro Tip:** For disputes involving closely-held South Dakota businesses, prioritize someone with direct experience applying GAAP to private company financials in state court. The valuation methodologies that hold up in Minnehaha County aren't always the ones that read well in a textbook.

## What to Expect

Engagements typically run $5,000 on the low end for a focused fraud review or straightforward damages calculation, up to $75,000 or more for complex commercial litigation involving multi-year financial reconstruction. Most Sioux Falls engagements land in the $15,000–40,000 range, with expert report delivery averaging 4–8 weeks from document production. Expect an initial retainer of $5,000–10,000 before work begins.

> **Reality Check:** The most common billing blowup is underestimating document volume. Attorneys describe "a few years of records" — the accountant scopes accordingly — and then a full decade of QuickBooks exports and bank statements arrives. Get a written scope of work that specifies exactly which documents are included before signing anything. Scope creep on a forensic engagement doesn't just cost money; it costs time you can't get back before trial.

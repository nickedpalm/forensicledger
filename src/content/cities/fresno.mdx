## How to Choose a Forensic Accountant in Fresno

- **Credential-match your matter type.** A CFF (Certified in Financial Forensics) is your default pick for litigation support and economic damages in commercial disputes. A CFE (Certified Fraud Examiner) is purpose-built for embezzlement and insurance fraud cases. For business valuation in divorce or shareholder disputes, filter for CVA or ABV holders. Don't hire a fraud specialist for a business interruption claim.
- **Ask for California CPA licensure, not just a national credential.** California's Board of Accountancy has its own continuing ed and disciplinary track. A lapsed or out-of-state CPA who happens to hold a CFE is not the same as a licensed California CPA with forensic credentials. Verify at the DCA license lookup before you call.
- **Prioritize deposition and trial testimony experience, not just report writing.** Fresno County Superior Court sees a high volume of agricultural business disputes and Central Valley commercial litigation — matters where a forensic accountant who has actually been cross-examined performs materially better than one who's only ever submitted reports. Ask for a case list, not just a CV.
- **Check for conflicts early.** Fresno's professional community is smaller than LA's or [San Francisco](/locations/california/san-francisco/)'s. The forensic accountant you want may have already been retained by opposing counsel in a related matter. Ask before you disclose case details.
- **Get a scope letter before any retainer conversation.** The engagement scope — whether that's transaction reconstruction, lost profits analysis, or expert report only — directly controls the fee. Firms that won't give you a written scope estimate before signing a retainer agreement are optimizing for their billing, not your case.

> **Pro Tip:** If your matter involves agricultural operations, agribusiness financing, or water rights disputes — all common in the Fresno market — ask specifically whether the accountant has experience with farm income statements and commodity pricing analysis. Generic forensic accounting experience doesn't substitute for sector fluency when opposing counsel's expert does.

## What to Expect

Forensic accounting engagements in Fresno typically run $5,000 on the low end (limited document review, narrow damages scope) to $75,000 or more for complex commercial litigation with multi-year transaction reconstruction and trial testimony. Most attorneys should budget $15,000–$35,000 for a contested commercial matter requiring a full expert report.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake is treating the initial retainer deposit as a fee cap. Forensic accountants bill hourly ($250–$500/hr for credentialed senior practitioners), and document complexity — not scope letters — drives the real number. The case that looks like a $10K engagement on day one often lands at $40K when the opposing party's records arrive in boxes. Build in a contingency.

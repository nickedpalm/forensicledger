## How to Choose a Forensic Accountant in San Antonio

- **Match credentials to case type.** A CFF (Certified in Financial Forensics) or CFE (Certified Fraud Examiner) is the right call for embezzlement and insurance fraud matters. If your case involves a business valuation dispute or divorce asset division, look for a CVA or ABV instead — they're trained specifically in valuation methodology, and opposing counsel will probe that gap if you hire the wrong specialist.
- **Verify active Texas CPA licensure.** All practicing forensic accountants in Texas must hold a current CPA license from the Texas State Board of Public Accountancy. An out-of-state credential alone won't hold up in a Bexar County courtroom — check TSBPA standing before you sign anything.
- **Ask about prior testimony in the 4th Court of Appeals district.** San Antonio's 4th Court handles a high volume of commercial and family law matters. An expert who understands local evidentiary standards and has been cross-examined in Texas discovery is worth more than a nationally recognized name who's never navigated a Bexar County deposition.
- **Request a redacted sample report.** A forensic accountant's expert report is their product. Ask for one from a comparable case before you retain them. If they balk, walk. If the report reads like a narrative without supporting workpapers and methodology disclosure, that's a problem you'll discover at deposition — not before.
- **Nail down the retainer structure in writing.** Most San Antonio forensic accounting firms bill hourly ($250–$450/hr for senior staff) against an upfront retainer. Some offer fixed-fee scopes for smaller insurance fraud matters. Ambiguity here creates real friction when the case drags into month five.

> **Pro Tip:** San Antonio's proximity to the Eagle Ford Shale region means a significant share of commercial disputes involve oil and gas royalty calculations and joint operating agreement disputes. If your matter touches energy, ask specifically whether the accountant has upstream or midstream experience — generalist commercial forensics won't get you there.

## What to Expect

Forensic accounting engagements in San Antonio typically run from $5,000 for a focused document review on a contained insurance claim up to $75,000 or more for complex commercial litigation requiring full damages reconstruction and trial testimony. Turnaround on an initial expert report is usually six to twelve weeks depending on document volume — expect the back end of that range if production is contested.

> **Reality Check:** The sticker shock usually hits at the engagement letter, not at final billing. The mistake most attorneys make is anchoring to a verbal estimate before anyone has actually reviewed the document set. Get a written estimate tied to specific deliverables after a real scoping call. "Comprehensive review" is not a deliverable.

## Local Market Overview

San Antonio's legal market runs deep on insurance defense, military contractor disputes, and family law — the city's massive JBSA (Joint Base San Antonio) footprint generates a steady docket of federal contractor fraud and government contract claims that reward forensic accountants with government auditing familiarity over pure commercial generalists. The region's dense healthcare sector, anchored by UT Health Science Center and a cluster of major regional hospital systems, also produces consistent revenue cycle fraud and qui tam cases that have made San Antonio one of the more active markets in Texas for financial forensics work.

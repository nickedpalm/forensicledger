Delaware's status as the incorporation capital of the United States means the legal disputes landing in Court of Chancery are routinely complex, high-stakes, and time-sensitive. ## How to Choose a Forensic Accountant in Wilmington

- **Match credentials to case type.** A CFF (Certified in Financial Forensics) is the right call for fraud and embezzlement work. If your case hinges on business valuation — a shareholder dispute or M&A earnout gone sideways — look for a CVA or ABV. Hiring the wrong credential for the case type is the most common mistake attorneys make.
- **Verify Court of Chancery experience specifically.** Delaware's Chancery Court has its own procedural rhythms and judicial expectations around expert testimony. An accountant who's testified in federal district court but never in Chancery may underestimate what that bench expects in a damages report.
- **Ask about opposing counsel conflicts upfront.** Wilmington's financial and legal community is tight. The same handful of forensic accounting firms appear on both sides of disputes in this market — get conflicts cleared before you have a substantive conversation.
- **Require a sample report or redacted engagement letter.** Anyone charging $300/hour should be able to show you what their expert report looks like. If they hedge, that's your answer.
- **Check whether they have litigation support staff or work solo.** Complex commercial disputes — especially those involving Delaware holding companies with subsidiaries in multiple jurisdictions — require document review bandwidth. A solo practitioner can be excellent; just know what you're getting.

> **Pro Tip:** Delaware's corporate transparency laws mean many of the financial records you'll need are publicly filed. A forensic accountant with specific experience pulling and reconciling Delaware entity filings (from the Division of Corporations) will move faster and bill fewer hours than someone learning that process on your engagement.

## What to Expect

Forensic accounting engagements in Wilmington typically run $5,000 on the low end for a contained insurance claim and up to $75,000 or more for complex commercial litigation involving multi-entity corporate structures — which is common here given the concentration of Delaware holding companies. Most engagements follow the same arc: document intake and preservation, financial reconstruction, damages quantification, draft report, and expert witness prep. Expect four to eight weeks for a full report on a mid-complexity matter, though expedited timelines are negotiable if your trial date is approaching.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake is treating the initial estimate as fixed. Forensic accounting fees expand with document volume and opposing counsel's cooperation level. Get a fee structure with defined hourly rates *and* a not-to-exceed estimate for the initial phase — then revisit scope before authorizing the full engagement.

That concentration of complex financial litigation means experienced forensic accountants here have seen deal structures and inter-company transaction schemes that practitioners in smaller markets simply haven't.

## How to Choose a Forensic Accountant in Birmingham

- **Verify the credential stack, not just CPA.** A CPA license is baseline. For litigation work, look for a CFF (Certified in Financial Forensics, issued by the AICPA) or CFE (Certified Fraud Examiner, via ACFE). For business valuation in divorce or commercial disputes, a CVA or ABV matters. Alabama doesn't require these specialty creds to testify, which means unqualified people do — check before you retain.
- **Ask for deposition and trial transcripts.** Birmingham's Jefferson County Circuit Court and Northern District of Alabama federal court have distinct rhythms. An expert who's testified in both handles cross-examination differently than one who's only written reports. Request case references from similar matters — commercial fraud, insurance disputes, or divorce financials, depending on your case type.
- **Match specialty to case type.** Fraud investigation (embezzlement, insurance fraud) and commercial damages analysis are different disciplines. A forensic accountant who spent 20 years doing fraud work for the FBI's Birmingham field office may not be the right fit for a business interruption claim. The overlap is smaller than most attorneys assume.
- **Get a conflict check upfront.** Birmingham's professional community is tight. The firm that audited your opponent's financials last year might be on your shortlist today. A thorough conflict screen before retention saves the nightmare of disqualification mid-case.
- **Clarify deliverable scope before engagement.** "Expert report" means different things to different practitioners. Confirm whether the engagement includes written report, rebuttal analysis, deposition prep, and trial testimony — and get each billed separately or itemized in the engagement letter.

> **Pro Tip:** Alabama Rule of Civil Procedure 26 governs expert disclosures. If your forensic accountant has never worked in Alabama state court, confirm they understand the disclosure timeline and report format requirements before you're stuck scrambling two weeks before the deadline.

## What to Expect

Forensic accounting engagements in Birmingham typically run $5,000–$75,000 depending on case complexity, volume of financial records, and whether trial testimony is included — a straight embezzlement investigation with clean books is closer to the low end; a contested commercial damages case with reconstructed transactions and a rebuttal report will approach the top. Most practitioners bill hourly ($250–$550/hr for credentialed experts) and require a retainer before review begins. Turnaround on initial analysis is typically four to eight weeks, with expedited timelines available at a premium.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake attorneys make is retaining the cheapest expert and then paying a second expert to fix the first report. A $3,500 engagement that produces a report opposing counsel dismantles on page two costs more than the $12,000 one that holds up. Scope and credential vetting up front is the cheaper path.

## How to Choose a Forensic Accountant in Dallas

- **Lead with credentials, not titles.** CFF (Certified in Financial Forensics) and CFE (Certified Fraud Examiner) are the two you're looking for on litigation matters. CVA or ABV matters if your case involves business valuation. Anyone without at least one of these has no business signing an expert report.
- **Match specialty to case type.** Fraud investigation, economic damages, and divorce/tracing are distinct disciplines. A forensic accountant who's spent 20 years on insurance claims may have no experience quantifying lost profits in a commercial dispute — ask specifically.
- **Verify Texas CPA licensure.** Texas requires active CPA licensure through the TSBPA for anyone signing attest-level work. Confirm their license is current at license.tsbpa.state.tx.us before engagement.
- **Ask about prior testimony in Dallas courts.** Northern District of Texas federal court and Dallas County district courts have different cultures around expert disclosure and Daubert challenges. Someone who's been cross-examined in both moves faster and costs less on rebuttal.
- **Get a conflict check in writing.** Dallas's legal and financial community is tight. Large commercial disputes involving Dallas-based companies can create unexpected conflicts at firms with Big Four or insurance-carrier relationships.

> **Pro Tip:** Ask for two or three anonymized case summaries that mirror your fact pattern. A competent forensic accountant should be able to describe their methodology, the damages model they used, and how opposing counsel attacked it — without revealing client identity.

## What to Expect

Forensic accounting engagements in Dallas typically run $5,000–$15,000 for straightforward fraud reviews or single-issue damages calculations, and $25,000–$75,000+ for complex commercial litigation with full expert reports, deposition prep, and trial testimony. Expect an initial document review phase of two to four weeks, followed by analysis and report drafting — rush timelines are possible but cost accordingly.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake attorneys make is hiring on hourly rate alone. A $350/hr forensic accountant who takes 200 hours to build a damages model you didn't need costs more — and hurts your case more — than a $500/hr specialist who delivers a court-ready report in 80. Scope the engagement before you negotiate the rate.
